The Concept of Anti-Sneeze

What Is Anti-Sneeze?


The anti-sneeze product reduces and controls sneezes and can include protecting against air-borne disease causing species, irritants, and pollutants. The product only requires a cotton swab, nose stick tube, or similar product with the anti-sneeze liquid composition that are both easily portable and convenient for daily use.

Why Anti-Sneeze?


A sneeze can produce up to 40,000 droplets, and the particles reach a distance of up to six feet. The droplets can last on surfaces from between two to eight hours. From the sneezes of unhealthy individuals, viruses can access into a respiratory system when the droplets reach the eyes, nose, or mouth. There are many places and situations where sneeze prevention is needed. The air is full of particles, microbes, and chemicals which have access to our respiratory system through our nose. Certain concerns include allergies, infections, invasive chemical species, and mold spores, among other ailments. Additionally, sneezes can transfer foreign particles and pathogens to others, possibly spreading illness. Therefore, an anti-sneeze product with the purpose of preventing the above measures would be beneficial.

Difference From Other Patents


There are other patents that functions around benefiting the effects and situations around sneezing, allergies, and related disease. Medical devices and compositions have been made for:
  • cleansing out harmful substances
  • treating certain infections and diseases, such as colds
  • nose congestion
  • helping deliver medication through the nose
  • and allergy related conditions

However, these agents, containing certain drugs, can have side effects such as blood pressure influence, sedation, depression, and dizziness, among other side effects. Specific drugs in the agents have different side effects and degrees of side effects depending on dosages, frequency of use, and duration. The anti-sneeze compositions are safe and effective, without any concerns to side effects, antibiotics, or systematic overuse of drugs. Additionally, these agents aid in effects from sneezing and/or deals with allergies and related diseases in relation to sneezing, but there is no treatment for sneeze reduction itself.
