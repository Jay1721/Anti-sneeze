Anti-Sneeze and COVID-19

COVID-19 and Its Effects

Since the end of 2019 through 2020, the COVID-19 disease has become a global pandemic. The disease is known to be transmitted through respiratory droplets, which can come into contact with the nose and mouth and spread the virus, or be inhaled into the lungs and upper respiratory tract (nose, mouth, and throat) where the virus can thrive. Respiratory droplets are spread by sneezing and coughing. During this time, regular nasal care is important in caring for one's well being and can be a large step in preventing and/or controlling attacks, infection, and spreading of the virus. In addition to health effects, COVID-19 has also damaged the economy, costing millions of Americans their jobs and a high unemployment rate comparable to the Great Depression.





Benefits of Anti-Sneeze

Anti-Sneeze Health Benefits

With this information in mind, the anti-sneeze product can be considered anti-pandemic. It brings benefit by:
  • Keeping in check and reducing transmission by controlling sneezes and maintaining respiratory health by limiting excess mucus buildup and minimize exposure to allergens
  • Targeting the inside of the body, unlike other treatments such as possible UV or sunlight treatments
  • Widening the nasal passages for air and ease up breathing, which can benefit the infected whose symptoms include breathing difficulties

With the reduction of excessive sneezing, this would greatly control the ability of the virus to be transmitted through respiratory droplets and reduce a chance for the virus to spread. Additionally, the virus can also be spread simply through breathing and talking; with preventative measures in mind, anti-sneeze can be used regularly to reduce the chance of infection from said transmissions.

Economic Benefits

The US Defense Production Act, which is distributing test swabs or other legal means could be used to produce anti-sneeze care. Ready availability of the product would encourage regular use of it, which furthermore can decrease the chance of infection in public places, including workplaces, This can decrease the chance for increased unemployment, as well as farm workers and similar workers who may be at increased risk of infection or contracting allergens or particles.





Benefits as Personal Product

Anti-sneeze can also benefit social distancing; individuals at home would not need to go out to get anti-sneeze treatment, which would be beneficial for professionals in health care settings which have been reported to have shortages in equipment for treatment; with it being better for people to ensure protective preventative care on their own, anti-sneeze would be a beneficial nasal and respiratory care and treatment product.










Benefits Alongside Vaccine

Since December 2020, the COVID-19 vaccine has come out. However, progress in distribution is slower than expected, with only 1 million out of expected 20 million people having received the vaccine as of December 23, 2020. There are still limited doses available, with only mostly politicians receiving the vaccine first. Since the vaccine is new, there are still unknowns about it and aspects that scientists still have to navigate. There have been side effects, such as fever or allergic reactions. Therefore, as the vaccines continue to be distributed and improved, any additional treatment would be beneficial to maintain and improve health. Anti-sneeze would be a large factor in helping preventative measures against infection through nasal and respiratory care. Through said preventative measures, anti-sneeze can greatly benefit those who have been infected from being infected again, with not much evidence suggesting the prevention of a second infection.
